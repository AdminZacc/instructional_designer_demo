The Design Challenge

Mid-level managers are often promoted for individual performance, not leadership readiness. The challenge was to close that gap — not with lectures about leadership theory, but with structured practice in the moments that matter most: difficult conversations, team breakdowns, ambiguous decisions, and cross-functional influence.

I designed this program to move participants from awareness to application, using a consistent cycle of instruction, scenario practice, real-world transfer, and group reflection.

The 6-Week Journey

01

Week One

Leadership Identity

Participants examine the shift from individual contributor to leader. They map their personal leadership style, identify default patterns under pressure, and articulate a clear leadership intention for the program.

  • Personal leadership style assessment
  • Reflection journal (Google Docs)
  • Peer intro exercise via Miro
Miro Google Docs
02

Week Two

Communication & Influence

Focuses on the mechanics of influence without authority. Learners practice structured feedback conversations, stakeholder framing, and active listening using a scenario-based case with a fictional team conflict.

  • Feedback scenario roleplay (Zoom breakouts)
  • Stakeholder communication plan template
  • Debrief discussion board
Zoom Google Docs
03

Week Three

High-Performance Teams

Explores team dynamics using Tuckman's stages as a framework for diagnosis. Participants map their own team, identify friction points, and design a short intervention to move through a stuck stage.

  • Team health diagnostic (Miro canvas)
  • Live facilitated session — group debrief
  • Intervention design brief
Miro Zoom
04

Week Four

Decision-Making Under Pressure

Learners work through high-stakes decision scenarios with incomplete information — simulating real manager conditions. Emphasis is on structured frameworks (WRAP, pre-mortem) over instinct alone.

  • Case-based decision scenario
  • Pre-mortem analysis worksheet
  • Async video reflection (Zoom clips)
Zoom Google Docs
05

Week Five

Leading Through Change

Covers the human side of change — resistance, loss, and trust repair. Participants draft a change communication plan for a real initiative they are currently managing or anticipating.

  • Change resistance mapping (Miro)
  • Live session — change Q&A fishbowl
  • Personal change communication plan
Miro Zoom
06

Week Six

Strategy, Vision & Transfer

Participants synthesize learning into a 90-day leadership action plan and present it to the cohort. Peer coaching pairs are established for post-program accountability.

  • 90-day leadership action plan
  • Cohort showcase presentations (Zoom)
  • Peer coaching pair kickoff
Zoom Google Docs

Facilitation & Transfer Design

Facilitation Guides

Structured, Flexible Facilitation

Each live session includes a detailed facilitation guide with time stamps, discussion anchors, and facilitator notes — built to support both new and experienced facilitators running the program.

Transfer Activities

On-the-Job Application

Every week includes a transfer activity that asks learners to apply a specific skill in their real work context within 48 hours — closing the gap between training and performance.

Peer Learning

Cohort-Driven Reflection

Miro canvases and Zoom breakouts create consistent space for peer exchange. Cohort learning reduces isolation and accelerates insight through shared experience and cross-functional perspective.

Key Design Decisions

⚙️

No Passive Content

Every instructional segment is tied to a decision, a reflection prompt, or an application task. Learners do not just consume — they produce.

🎯

Real Work as the Practice Field

Transfer activities use participants' actual teams and live projects — not hypothetical simulations — so application is immediate and measurable.

📐

Low-Tool, High-Fidelity

Google Docs, Miro, and Zoom were chosen deliberately — tools the organization already had, so no tech friction blocked learning.

🔄

Spaced Repetition Built In

Each week's opening revisits and builds on the prior week's transfer activity, reinforcing key concepts without redundant re-teaching.
