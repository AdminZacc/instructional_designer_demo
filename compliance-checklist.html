Job Aid · Visual Design

Quick Reference — Compliance Checklist

A one-page visual job aid designed to reduce compliance errors at the point of performance. Deployed across three departments — 30% fewer compliance incidents in the first quarter post-launch.

Tools: Adobe InDesign, Canva Format: Print + Digital PDF Audience: Front-line staff

Project Snapshot

Problem

Staff were missing required compliance steps not from lack of training, but from lack of access to the right information at the right moment. Training alone wasn't the answer.

Design Goal

Build a zero-cognitive-load reference tool that surfaces critical compliance steps in under 10 seconds — without requiring staff to leave their workflow.

Outcome

  • 30% reduction in compliance errors (Q1 post-launch)
  • Adopted across 3 departments
  • Requested by staff as a laminated desk copy

Compliance Checklist

Front-Line Staff · Version 2.1

0%

Complete

01

Before You Begin

  • ID + one secondary identifier required
  • Must be on file before service initiation
  • Do not proceed if flag is unresolved
02

During the Interaction

  • No retroactive entries permitted
  • See code reference on reverse side
  • No exceptions approved at staff level
  • Read from script if client requests
03

Before You Close

  • Unsaved records auto-delete after 30 min
  • Scanned copies accepted
  • Follow-up window is 5 business days
⚠️

Critical Escalation Triggers

If any of the following occur, stop the interaction and contact your supervisor immediately: client identity cannot be verified · consent is refused or withdrawn · system flags an active hold · client requests a policy exception.

Approved by Compliance Office · Revised Q1 2025 · Questions? Contact compliance@org.gov

Design Decisions

🧠

Cognitive Load First

I limited each section to a maximum of four items. Research on working memory (Miller's Law) informed grouping so staff could scan, act, and move on — not read a wall of text mid-interaction.

🗂️

Sequential Not Categorical

Items are ordered by workflow sequence — Before, During, Close — matching the mental model staff already use. Categorical grouping (e.g., by policy type) was tested first and caused more errors, not fewer.

⚠️

Escalation Triggers Separated

Critical escalation conditions are visually isolated in an alert block rather than buried in the checklist. Staff need to recognize these instantly — contrast, color, and icon separation make them unmissable.

📄

Print + Digital Parity

The original was designed in InDesign for print-first production, then exported as an accessible PDF. Layout decisions — column width, font size, spacing — were validated on both a laminated A4 card and a tablet screen.
