Interactive Media & Educational Technology

Interactive Education — HTML5 Knowledge Checks & Animation

Blending pedagogy with pixels: custom HTML5 knowledge check modules and Adobe After Effects animations that make complex health and science concepts engaging, accessible, and measurable.

Tools: HTML5, Adobe After Effects, Canva Audience: General public, community health learners Format: Self-paced interactive + video

Project Snapshot

The design challenge, approach, and measurable outcomes for this interactive media collection.

Problem

Learners disengaged from text-heavy health education content. Passive reading produced low retention and near-zero behavior transfer. Complex science topics needed a visual language to become accessible to non-clinical audiences.

Design Goal

Produce interactive HTML5 knowledge checks that keep learners active in the content, and pair them with short animated explainers that replace walls of text with motion, metaphor, and narrative.

Outcomes

  • Measurable increase in post-module quiz completion rates
  • Animated explainers averaged 80%+ play-through rate
  • Learners self-reported higher confidence after interactive content
  • Reusable component library reduced production time for future modules

HTML5 Knowledge Checks

How the custom HTML5 components were designed and why they outperform standard quiz tools for this audience.

Design Principles

  • Immediate feedback: Correct and incorrect responses trigger contextual coaching, not just a red X
  • Low stakes: No score reporting to create psychological safety for exploration
  • Mobile-first: Touch-friendly tap targets and responsive layouts for phone-based learners
  • Plain language: Question stems written at a 7th-grade reading level

Technical Build

  • Vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no framework dependency
  • Accessible: keyboard-navigable, ARIA labels, high-contrast mode compatible
  • Embeddable in any CMS, LMS, or static site via iframe or direct include
  • State persisted via sessionStorage for multi-page module flows

Benefit: A learner on a low-end Android phone at a rural health fair gets the same experience as a desktop user in a clinic.

Animation & Motion Design

How Adobe After Effects animations are used to replace text-heavy explanations with visual narratives.

Explainer Animations

Short 60–90 second motion graphics segments introduce complex concepts — social determinants of health, medication adherence cycles, rural health access barriers — before learners engage with the knowledge checks.

Production Workflow

Script → Storyboard → Asset design in Canva/Illustrator → Animation in After Effects → Compressed MP4 export → Embedded in module with captions and transcript.

Accessibility

Every animation includes closed captions, a downloadable transcript, and a static image fallback for low-bandwidth environments — meeting WCAG 2.1 AA requirements.

ID Connection: Why Interaction Matters

Active Recall Over Passive Reading

Research consistently shows that retrieval practice — being asked to recall information rather than re-read it — produces stronger long-term retention. Every knowledge check in this collection was designed as a retrieval event, not a comprehension check. The goal is to make the learner's brain do the work, not just confirm they read the page.

Cognitive Load Management

Animations reduce extraneous cognitive load by externalizing the mental model. Instead of asking learners to construct a mental image from text, the animation builds it for them — freeing working memory for the deeper processing the knowledge check then demands.
